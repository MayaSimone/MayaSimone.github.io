2x2x2 Rubik's Cube Solver

As part of MakeHarvard 2020, a 24-hour make-a-thon at Harvard University, my team decided to design and build a robot that could solver a 2x2x2 Rubik's cube. The robot used an Arduino to control continuous rotation servos which could lift, flip, rotate, and twist the block as well as color sensors to
read the initial scrambled state of the block. The scrambled state information was passed
to a Python solving algorithm designed by one of my teammates before the solving
instructions were sent back to the Arduino.

I mostly worked on the mechanical aspects of the robot, focused on physically manipulating the block. We were able to create the necessary motions via two main components: a 3D printed claw on a rack and pinion mechanism and a 3D printed based mounted on a servo to fociliate turning and also flipping the block. This project won the MakeHarvard Original Award 2020, given to the best project made only of materials provided at the event.
